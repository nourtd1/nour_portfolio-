# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Clients, founders, and startups evaluating Nour as a contractor or hire. They arrive with a specific question: can this person ship real products? They are scanning quickly for proof of work, technical range, and enough personality to want to work with them. Secondary audience: other developers and tech-adjacent collaborators who already know what they're looking at and want to see depth.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Annour Mahamat Abdoulaye (Nour'dev), Software Engineer and Mobile/Web Developer based in Gisenyi, Rwanda. The portfolio is split into two surfaces: a 3D WebGL room (Three.js) that acts as the first impression and frame, and an OS-style 2D desktop (React) rendered inside the monitor via iframe that contains the actual content (projects, experience, contact).

The experience is the portfolio. A visitor's reaction to the 3D room is the brand statement; the inner OS content delivers the proof of work. Success looks like a visitor who books a call or sends an email because the portfolio demonstrated both craft and capability.

## Brand Personality

Sharp · Technical · Confident

Voice: direct and specific. No aphorisms, no "passionate about building things." Every word describes something real. The retro-computer aesthetic is a deliberate identity statement, not nostalgia — it signals precision, systems thinking, and someone who knows what they're building and why.

Emotional goal: make the visitor feel they're looking at the work of someone who ships. Not inspires, not disrupts — ships.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS-cream landing page**: warm off-white background, Inter on everything, floating cards, hero metrics (8 products / 4 apps), gradient text. The 2025 AI template. The project has these numbers; they must not be displayed like a SaaS pricing section.
- **Dark editorial/dev blog**: pure black + IBM Plex Mono + minimal everything. Looks like a GitHub README, not a portfolio. The mono aesthetic is legitimate here but can't be the entire vocabulary.
- **Flashy agency-style showreel**: scroll-jacked, over-animated, more motion than content. Hard to navigate, style over signal.
- **Over-designed AI portfolio**: gradient text, glassmorphism, hero metrics displayed as stat cards, numbered section eyebrows (01/02/03), identical card grids, tracked all-caps kickers above every section.

## Design Principles

1. **The experience is the first credential.** The 3D room is not a loader — it is the opening argument. A visitor who navigates the room has already seen craft before opening a single app.
2. **Retro with intent, not nostalgia.** The BIOS boot and OS desktop are precision choices, not costume. Every retro element earns its place by referencing real computing history (green phosphor, monospace readouts) rather than applying "retro" as decoration.
3. **Signal over spectacle.** The inner OS content must be readable, scannable, and credible. Visual effects in the 3D shell; clarity and hierarchy in the content windows.
4. **Specificity as voice.** Nour is from Chad, building in Rwanda, with 4 published apps and a specific client list. The design should make that specific rather than universal. No softening, no "passionate about technology."
5. **Committed palette, not hedged.** The color strategy is not warm-neutral safety. The terminal green (#00C896) and black are the brand. Supporting colors extend from that anchor, not away from it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The 3D WebGL room is an enhanced experience; the inner OS desktop (Portfolio Hub, Terminal) must meet AA contrast, keyboard navigability, and focus states. The loading screen already offers a direct "OPEN PORTFOLIO" escape path for users who cannot or prefer not to use the 3D experience — preserve and surface this clearly. Reduced-motion alternatives for all entrance animations in the inner site.
